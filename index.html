About

Researcher, engineer, builder

I am a Doctoral Student in Machine Learning and a Fellow at the ETH AI Center in Zurich, advised by Fanny Yang. I work on the methodological challenges behind AI systems that need to remain robust when the stakes are high, from memorization in language models to privacy-preserving data release and hidden confounding in observational studies.

Alongside my PhD, I have worked at Apple on image and video generation for Apple Intelligence and at Google DeepMind on student-teacher distillation with the Gemma post-training team. Before ETH, I worked on conformal prediction and interpretability at Cambridge and developed machine learning systems for fighting financial crime at Featurespace.

Research

Three threads, one goal: machine learning you can trust

Foundation models that behave better

I study memorization, regurgitation, and post-training dynamics in foundation models, with an emphasis on techniques that improve safety without making models unusable.

Copyright-Protected Language Generation via Adaptive Model Fusion

Privacy-preserving machine learning

I work on methods for releasing or learning from sensitive data while retaining practical utility, especially in settings where privacy is a hard requirement rather than a nice-to-have.

PrivPGD for differentially private data synthesis

Causal inference under hidden bias

I develop methods for identifying treatment effects and quantifying hidden confounding, especially when real-world decisions depend on observational data that cannot be trusted at face value.
